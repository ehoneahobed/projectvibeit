# Day 1: Project Planning

Today is all about laying the foundation for your project. We'll choose your business concept, plan your content structure, and set up your development environment. This planning phase is crucial - it will guide every decision you make throughout the project.

## Step 1: Choose Your Business Concept

### Business Selection Criteria

When choosing your business concept, consider:

1. **Personal Interest**: Pick something you're genuinely interested in
2. **Market Research**: Look at existing businesses in that space
3. **Content Potential**: Ensure there's enough content to create a full website
4. **Visual Appeal**: Choose something that will look good visually

### Business Ideas (Choose One)

#### Option 1: Local Coffee Shop - "Brew & Bean"
**Target Audience**: Coffee lovers, remote workers, local community
**Key Pages**: Home, Menu, About, Location, Contact
**Visual Elements**: Coffee photos, warm colors, cozy atmosphere

#### Option 2: Freelance Photographer - "Capture Moments"
**Target Audience**: Couples, families, businesses needing professional photos
**Key Pages**: Home, Portfolio, Services, About, Contact
**Visual Elements**: Photography portfolio, elegant design, testimonials

#### Option 3: Small Consultancy - "Strategic Solutions"
**Target Audience**: Small businesses, startups, entrepreneurs
**Key Pages**: Home, Services, Case Studies, Team, Contact
**Visual Elements**: Professional imagery, clean design, trust indicators

#### Option 4: Local Bakery - "Sweet Dreams"
**Target Audience**: Local customers, event planners, dessert lovers
**Key Pages**: Home, Products, Custom Orders, About, Contact
**Visual Elements**: Food photography, warm colors, mouth-watering images

## Step 2: Define Your Target Audience

### Audience Research Questions

Answer these questions for your chosen business:

1. **Who is your primary customer?**
   - Age range
   - Income level
   - Location
   - Interests

2. **What problems do they have?**
   - What are they looking for?
   - What frustrates them?
   - What motivates them to buy?

3. **How do they find businesses like yours?**
   - Google search
   - Social media
   - Word of mouth
   - Local directories

## Step 3: Content Planning

### Content Structure

Create a content outline for your website:

#### Homepage
- Hero section with compelling headline
- Brief introduction to the business
- Key services or products
- Call-to-action (contact, book, etc.)

#### About Page
- Business story and mission
- Team member profiles (if applicable)
- Values and approach
- Why choose this business

#### Services/Products Page
- Detailed service descriptions
- Pricing information
- Process explanation
- Examples of work

#### Contact Page
- Contact form
- Location and hours
- Phone and email
- Social media links

### Content Creation Strategy

1. **Write Key Copy Sections**
   - Hero headline (compelling and clear)
   - About section (story and value proposition)
   - Service descriptions (benefits-focused)
   - Contact information

2. **Plan Visual Content**
   - Hero image
   - Product/service photos
   - Team photos (if applicable)
   - Location photos

3. **Define Call-to-Actions**
   - Primary CTA (e.g., "Book Now", "Get Quote")
   - Secondary CTAs (e.g., "Learn More", "Contact Us")

## Step 4: Competitor Research

### Research Your Competitors

Find 3-5 similar businesses and analyze their websites:

1. **What works well?**
   - Design elements you like
   - Content that's effective
   - Features that stand out

2. **What could be improved?**
   - Design issues
   - Missing information
   - Poor user experience

3. **Gap Analysis**
   - What are they missing?
   - What can you do better?
   - Unique selling points

### Research Questions

- How do they present their services?
- What's their pricing strategy?
- How do they handle contact/booking?
- What's their brand personality?
- How do they build trust?

## Step 5: Technical Planning

### Project Structure

Set up your project folder structure:

```
project-1-static-website/
├── index.html
├── pages/
│   ├── about.html
│   ├── services.html
│   └── contact.html
├── css/
│   ├── style.css
│   ├── responsive.css
│   └── components.css
├── js/
│   ├── main.js
│   └── forms.js
├── images/
│   ├── hero.jpg
│   ├── about.jpg
│   └── services/
├── assets/
│   ├── favicon.ico
│   └── robots.txt
└── README.md
```

### Technology Stack

- **HTML5**: Semantic structure
- **CSS3**: Styling and responsive design
- **Vanilla JavaScript**: Interactivity and forms
- **Form Handling**: Formspree, Netlify Forms, or similar
- **Hosting**: Netlify, Vercel, or GitHub Pages

## Step 6: Development Environment Setup

### Required Tools

1. **Code Editor**: VS Code (recommended)
2. **Version Control**: Git and GitHub
3. **Browser**: Chrome with DevTools
4. **Image Editor**: Canva, Figma, or similar
5. **AI Assistant**: ChatGPT, Claude, or GitHub Copilot

### Setup Steps

1. **Install VS Code**
   - Download from https://code.visualstudio.com/
   - Install useful extensions (Live Server, Prettier, etc.)

2. **Set up Git Repository**
   ```bash
   mkdir project-1-static-website
   cd project-1-static-website
   git init
   ```

3. **Create Project Structure**
   - Create all the folders and files listed above
   - Set up basic HTML template
